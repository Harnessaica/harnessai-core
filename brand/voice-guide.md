# HarnessAI — Voice Guide

## The Rule
If a grain farmer or plumbing contractor can't understand it, rewrite it.

## What We Sound Like
Plain spoken. Confident. Zero jargon.

We're a business owner talking to business owners. We've done the hard work of figuring out what actually moves the needle — and we say so plainly.

## Good vs. Bad Examples

| Bad | Good |
|---|---|
| "Leverage cutting-edge AI to optimize your operational workflows." | "Cut your invoice processing time from 2 hours to 10 minutes." |
| "We deliver transformative solutions for your business ecosystem." | "We implement one AI tool that saves you real time. That's it." |
| "Our robust platform seamlessly integrates with your existing stack." | "It works with the tools you already use." |
| "Unlock the power of artificial intelligence for your enterprise." | "AI tools that work as hard as you do." |

## Words We Never Use
- leverage, synergy, robust, seamless, cutting-edge, transformative, ecosystem
- revolutionize, disrupt, unlock, empower
- "at this point in time," "circle back," "touch base"
- Any phrase that sounds like it came from a Silicon Valley pitch deck

## Sentence Rules
- Short. One idea per sentence.
- Active voice. "We build X" not "X is built by us."
- Specific over vague. "$500 flat fee" not "affordable pricing."
- Lead with the result: "Save 2 hours a week" not "Our tool helps you potentially save time."

## Tone by Context

**Website copy:** Direct and confident. A clear offer, a clear CTA. No fluff.

**Proposals:** Like a conversation. "Here's what I heard. Here's what I'd do. Here's what it costs."

**Client reports:** Plain language results. "You saved X hours this month" not "significant efficiency gains were realized."

**Emails:** Short. One idea. Sign off with Cheers.
